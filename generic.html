Anyways, a little bit about myself, I am an undergraduate student at the University of Toronto specializing in Mathematics and its Applications in Statistics/Probability and finished a major in Actuarial Science. I started off my undergrad majoring in Actuarial Science with the goal of becoming an actuary, although I did really well in actuarial science courses and I could probably take the exams, being an actuary required other skills so I weighed the pros and cons and wanted to have more career choices in the future and potentially go into grad school to do something I am passionate about, so I majored in Statistics. There were overlaps between actuarial science courses and statistics so the addition of a statistics major was easy. The completed actuarial science major is still useful though, since I learned finanicial concepts like derivatives, markets, etc which would be helpful in future data analysis roles in financial companies. I also got a taste of applied statistics and worked with data in finance. Then during third year, I took this course called introduction to abstract mathematics and some other math course and got addicted to math. "addicted" as in something I could sit all day and think about and did really well in. So I chose the specialist in mathematics & its applications in statistics/probability program in the end which is an enhanced statistics and math double major. I like the rigour in math and enjoy the beauty of it, but I would prefer applying it to things as it opens up more career opportunities than to study math for the sake of it (pure maths).
